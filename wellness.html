Ayurveda and Wellness: The Science of Life

Ayurveda is not just a system of medicine; it is a way of life. Originating in India over 5,000 years ago, Ayurveda emphasizes that wellness is the dynamic integration of environment, body, mind, and spirit.

The Three Doshas

According to Ayurveda, everything in the universe is made up of five elements: space, air, fire, water, and earth. These elements combine to form three metabolic types or "Doshas" in the human body:

  • Vata (Space & Air): Controls bodily functions associated with motion, including blood circulation, breathing, and heartbeat.
  • Pitta (Fire & Water): Controls the body's metabolic systems, including digestion, absorption, and body temperature.
  • Kapha (Water & Earth): Controls growth in the body. It supplies water to all body parts, moisturizes the skin, and maintains the immune system.

Daily Routine (Dinacharya)

To maintain absolute wellness, Ayurveda recommends following a Dinacharya (daily routine). Waking up before sunrise, scraping the tongue, oil pulling, and practicing Yoga or meditation are key steps to keeping your doshas balanced.
